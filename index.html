About

I'm a product designer at Plum, working on financial products. I focus on the behaviours that help people build better money habits.

I like making complicated things feel obvious to the people using them. I care about shipping quickly and measuring honestly.

I'm drawn to challenging, ambiguous problems. I use research, product thinking, and collaboration to make them clearer.

Questions I help teams answer

How do we make complex products feel simple, trusted, and easy to control?

I start by understanding the underlying system, then reduce the experience to the decisions users actually need to make. I make costs, consequences, and next steps visible, especially when money is involved.

How do we create experiences that feel personal without making the product harder to scale?

I look for differences that genuinely change what users need, such as their goals, confidence, eligibility, or behaviour. Then I design shared journeys that adapt through content, logic, and progressive disclosure.

How do we balance customer needs, business goals, regulation, and technology?

I make the constraints explicit early and use them to shape the solution, not limit it at the end. I work with product, engineering, data, and compliance to decide where we can simplify, where we need safeguards, and what trade-offs are worth making.

How do we know whether a solution is changing behaviour and driving impact?

I define the behaviour the product should influence and connect it to clear signals, such as activation, completion, repeat use, drop-off, retention, or support contact. Then I test the riskiest assumptions before investing in the full solution.
