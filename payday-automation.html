Payday Improvements

Role
Product Designer, end-to-end product ownership
Team
1 PM, 1 data analyst, 2 frontend, 3 backend engineers
Timeframe
2 sprints, shipped Q2 2026

Overview

Making Plum’s best saving rule easier to use

Payday was already Plum’s highest-impact savings rule, with users saving around £80 each month on average.

The opportunity was not creating demand. It was removing friction from discovery and setup.

The problem

A valuable feature with unnecessary friction

Payday already had strong adoption, with more than 250K users.

But it was hard to find, and harder than other rules to set up.

Hard to discover

Payday was not part of onboarding and had low visibility inside the app.

Hard to complete

The setup required too many decisions, and only around 30% of users completed it.

Problem screenshot

Goals

Move Payday from a high-value but underused rule into a core saving habit.

For the business, this meant growing Payday share from 45% to 50% of automation-active users, while increasing deposits and AUM.

For users, this meant making Payday easier to discover, quicker to set up, and less effortful to trust.

Research insights

01

Payday already had product-market fit

250K+ users had already enabled Payday without onboarding.

7 out of 10 interview participants were already using it.

The problem was not demand. It was activation.

Source: Automation usage data and New Pockets interviews.
02

Setup friction was blocking adoption

Payday had the longest setup of any savings rule.

Only around 30% of users completed the flow.

Confidence and ease of setup also scored only average.

Source: Setup funnel data, SUS survey, and app usability study.
03

Discovery mattered as much as the flow

Changing the Home entry point reduced Payday conversion.

The old “Get Started” panel converted at 33%.

The new Home carousel converted at 26%.

Surface and framing influenced behaviour as much as UX.

Source: Adoption analysis and pre-launch funnel data.

Design decisions

Decision 1

Reduce setup effort

Why: We believed fewer decisions would increase completion.

We reduced setup to two inputs and added smart defaults.

Decision screenshot
Decision 2

Improve discoverability

Why: We believed earlier visibility would increase adoption.

We introduced Payday in the onboarding automation step.

Decision screenshot
Decision 3

Keep the experience familiar

Why: We believed consistency would reduce uncertainty.

We aligned the onboarding and in-app setup patterns.

Decision screenshot

Impact

Weekly Payday adoption increased from 55% to 64%.
Monthly Payday users grew from 250K to 265K.
Setup conversion increased from 30% to 39%.
Payday AUM grew from under £19M to over £22M.
All-rules automation adoption increased by 22%.

The honest miss: setup conversion improved, but stayed relatively low. It showed that simplifying the flow was not enough on its own.

Takeaways

Optimising the flow was not enough

Simplifying setup helped, but only after users reached the flow.

Next time, I would spend more time finding the best moment to nudge users to automate.

Discovery needed to be more contextual

Onboarding helped, but it is only one moment in the journey.

Next time, I would explore prompts based on user behaviour, not only fixed app surfaces.

Measurement needed to come earlier

Tracking and GTM were still being defined close to launch.

Next time, I would align events, success metrics, and launch plans before implementation.
