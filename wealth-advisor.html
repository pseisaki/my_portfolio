Wealth Advisor Plan Builder

Role
Product Designer, sole designer on this project
Team
1 PM, 1 lead engineer, 1 data analyst, 2 frontend, 3 backend (AI squad)
Timeframe
3 sprints, shipped June, in active iteration
This product is live and iterating. Numbers below reflect Q2 results, Q3 changes are in progress.

The problem

85% of eligible users never opened the advisor

Two stacked gaps were compounding: a discovery gap (most users never found the advisor at all) and a completion gap (of those who did start, 9 in 10 never actually saw a finished plan).

  • Retirement questions asked sensitive financial info too early, before trust was established
  • Investment conversations deflected to "emergency fund" instead of answering the actual question
  • General saving never asked "saving for what," so plans felt generic
  • Plans appeared as unreadable chat text, not as a distinct, findable artifact

Goal

Move plan save rate from a ~12% baseline toward a 30% target. Fix visibility so plans stop disappearing into chat text.

Process

Before designing, ran concept testing with interviewers on early plan concepts, fielded a 200-participant survey with financial advisors to validate demand and framing, and ran a usability test on the resulting flow.

Decision 1

Turn the plan from chat text into a tappable card, leading to a full plan detail screen

Why: 43% of users who received a plan never saw it. It was surfaced as plain chat text, not as UI.
Before: plan shown as wall of chat text
After: tappable plan card and detail screen
Decision 2

Move setup actions (payday saver, ISA open) to the top of the plan detail screen

Why: users needed the "what do I do next" moment immediately, not buried below the plan explanation.
Layout before/after: setup actions repositioned to top
Decision 3

Add a homepage "Start a Plan" entry point

Why: the discovery gap was structural. The advisor had no persistent, visible entry point anywhere in the app.
Homepage placement of "Start a Plan" entry point

Results

Sessions nearly doubled week-on-week post-launch: 3,343 to 11,942.
Plan save rate climbed from a 12% baseline to a 15.2% peak.
Plan Viewed to Saved conversion improved from about 48% to 54.4%.
CRM campaign drove +140% more planning sessions vs. control, with 85% first-time planners.
Honest miss: OKRs landed below target this quarter, funded plans reached 11k against a 30k goal, AUM reached £12M against a £45M goal. The discovery and completion fixes above are the lever for closing that gap.

Framing: this is a discovery and funnel story, not a quality one. The product works once people actually reach it.

Reflection

The retirement questionnaire fix shipped too late to show up in Q2 data. Q3 will show its real effect.

Next: general saving still needs a "what are you saving for" step before generating a plan. The investment agent is still broken (87% failure rate, pivoting everything to emergency fund) and is the next priority.

Currently iterating on Q3 changes based on this Q2 data.
